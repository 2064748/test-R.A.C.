Samsø Island ⋄ Denmark

Denmark

Did you know that Denmark generated half of its electricity from wind and solar power in 2020? That’s the highest number ever.

Denmark has been ranking high in the list of sustainable countries for decades. The Danes produce a lot of green energy and are always exploring alternatives to fossil fuels.

Denmark loves clean, renewable energy. Today, 50% of electricity in Denmark is supplied by wind and solar power. Wind power is one of the mostly used type of renewable energy in Denmark.

Denmark also has an island called Samsø. This island switched from fossil fuels to generated, renewable energy in a period of 10 years. Today, the island of Samsø can take pride in its unique title as Denmark’s Renewable Energy Island.

Samsø Island

The world’s first energy islands will be constructed in Denmark in 2026. The project will be able to leverage the enormous wind resources in the North and Baltic seas.

The energy islands mark the beginning of a new era for the generation of energy from offshore wind, aimed at creating a green energy supply for Danish and foreign electricity grids.

These energy islands are designed to act as hubs for connecting the energy generated from offshore wind to the energy systems in the region around the two seas.

The islands, that act as green power plants at sea, are expected to play an important role in Denmark’s and Europe’s transition away from fossil fuel energy sources. Samsø Island’s total surface is comparable to almost 100 olympic-sized swimming pools.

How does it work?

  • There is wind.

  • The mechanical energy is converted into electrical energy.

  • The transformer ensures that the alternating current is converted into high voltage.

  • The generated energy is then transported via cables located in the mast to the electricity grid for use.

  • This sustainable energy is used by us, for instance for heating and electricity.
